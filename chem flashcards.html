Alkane halogenation

(free radical substitution with halogen)

RX + X₂ → RX + HX

Reagent: halogen (Cl₂, Br₂, I₂)

Conditions: UV light

Product: halogenoalkane

Alkene halogenation

(electrophilic addition with halogen)

R=R' + X₂ → X-R-R'-X

Reagent: halogen (Cl₂, Br₂, I₂)

Conditions: room temperature

Product: halogenoalkane

Electrophilic addition of alkene with hydrogen halide

R=R' + HX → H-R-R'-X

Reagent: hydrogen halide (HCl, HBr, HI)

Product: halogenoalkane

Electrophilic addition of alkene to form alkyl hydrogensulfate

R=R' + H₂SO₄ → H-R-R'-OSO₃H

Reagent: concentrated sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄)

Conditions: room temperature

Product: alkyl hydrogensulfate

Hydration of alkene

R=R' + H₂O(g) ‒(H₃PO₄)→ H-R-R'-OH

Reagent: steam, phosphoric acid catalyst (H₃PO₄)

Conditions: 300°c, 60atm

Product: alcohol

Hydrolysis of halogenoalkane

(nucleophilic substitution with hydroxide)

RX + MOH → ROH + MX

Reagent: aqueous hydroxides (NaOH(aq) or KOH(aq))

Conditions: warm

Product: alcohol

Nucleophilic substitution of halogenoalkane to form nitrile

RX + MCN → RCN + MX

Reagent: cyanides (NaCN or KCN)

Conditions: ethanolic, warm

Product: nitrile

Nucleophilic substitution of halogenoalkane to form amine

RX + 2NH₃ → RNH₂ + NH₄⁺X⁻

Reagent: excess ammonia

Conditions: ethanolic, sealed tube

Product: amine

Halogenoalkane elimination

Reagent: hydroxides (NaOH or KOH)

Conditions: ethanolic, reflux

Product: alkene

Alcohol dehydration (elimination)

R-R'-OH ‒(H₂SO₄)→ R=R' + H₂O

Reagent: concentrated sulfuric acid

Conditions: heat

Product: alkene

Nucleophilic addition of aldehyde to alcohol

RCHO + 2[H] → RCH₂OH

Reagent: sodium borohydride (NaBH₄)

Conditions: aqueous solution

Product: primary alcohol

Nucleophilic addition of aldehyde to hydroxy nitrile

RCHO + HCN → RC(H)(OH)CN

Reagent: NaCN or KCN then dilute acid

Product: hydroxy nitrile

Nucleophilic addition of ketone to alcohol

RCOR' + 2[H] → RC(OH)(H)R'

Reagent: sodium borohydride (NaBH₄)

Conditions: aqueous solution

Product: secondary alcohol

Nucleophilic addition of ketone to hydroxy nitrile

RCOR' + HCN → RC(OH)(R')CN

Reagent: NaCN or KCN then dilute acid

Product: hydroxy nitrile

Carboxylic acid esterification

RCOOH + R'OH → RCOOR' + H₂O

Reagent: alcohol, conc H₂SO₄(aq) catalyst

Conditions: hot

Product: ester

Acid anhydride esterification

RCOOCOR' + R"OH → RCOOR" + R'COOH

Reagent: alcohol

Conditions: warm

Product: ester (and carboxylic acid)

Acid anhydride hydrolysis

RCOOCOR' + H₂O → 2RCOOH

Reagent: water

Product: carboxylic acid

Acid anhydride to form amide

RCOOCOR' + NH₃ → RCONH₂ + R'COOH

Reagent: ammonia

Product: amide & carboxylic acid

Acid anhydride to form N-alkylamide

RCOOCOR' + R"NH₂ → RCONHR" + R'COOH

Reagent: primary amine

Product: N-alkylamide & carboxylic acid

Acyl chloride esterification

(nucleophilic addition-elimination with alcohol)

RCOCL + R'OH → RCOOR' + HCl

Reagent: alcohol

Conditions: hot

Product: ester
